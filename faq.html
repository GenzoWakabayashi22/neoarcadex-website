Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about NeoArcadeX

NeoArcadeX is a native macOS frontend for managing and launching retro games via RetroArch. It downloads covers, screenshots, videos, and metadata from ScreenScraper or from a dedicated media server, with a modern SwiftUI interface.

macOS 11.0 (Big Sur) or later, 8 GB RAM (16 GB recommended), ~500 MB for the app plus additional space for ROMs and artwork/video. See the Requirements page for details.

17 classic systems including Neo Geo AES/CD, Arcade (MAME set), Amiga, PlayStation, Saturn, SNES, Mega Drive, Sega 32X, Sega CD, Dreamcast, PC‑Engine / PC‑Engine CD, 3DO, Game Boy, Game Boy Advance, and Jaguar. NeoArcadeX selects optimal RetroArch cores automatically.

Yes. NeoArcadeX is a frontend and requires RetroArch. The app checks RetroArch and required cores and guides you if anything is missing.

The trial lasts 7 days with server verification and a 24‑hour offline grace period. Pro unlock uses a code (email + server‑side secret) and grants a permanent license stored in Keychain. After activation, it works offline.

It’s recommended for metadata and artwork. NeoArcadeX adapts to Free and Premium accounts with rate limiting and caching. Alternatively, you can download media from our dedicated server with no daily quota.

Absolutely. You can override artwork and game info directly from your library. Thumbnails are regenerated and local edits are preserved.

No. By default, we do not send telemetry. The app contacts servers only to verify trial/license and to fetch metadata/media if you choose to use those features. Email is used only if you subscribe to the newsletter.

Large images (ISO/CHD 2–4GB) can take minutes to hash. NeoArcadeX minimizes SHA1 usage and falls back to ROM name when possible, especially for Arcade/cartridge systems.
